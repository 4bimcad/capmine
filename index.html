How is investor capital protected under Peruvian law?

Investor capital in Peru is protected through a robust legal framework anchored in the Constitution (Article 62°) and specialized mining legislation. The Mining Investment Law (No. 27623) guarantees 30+ year concession rights registered in the National Mining Cadastre, while stabilization clauses shield against regulatory changes. Peru's 35+ bilateral investment treaties and free currency convertibility (Decree Law No. 637) ensure financial protections, backed by international arbitration recognition. Operationally, projects utilize MIGA political risk insurance from the World Bank, escrow accounts in AA-rated banks, and 5% performance bonds - a system that has delivered 18-22% annual returns with full capital recovery since 2018. The Investment Promotion Law (No. 662) further reinforces these safeguards, making Peru one of Latin America's most secure mining jurisdictions.

What are the key regulations for mining in Peru?

Peru's mining sector operates under a comprehensive legal framework established by the General Mining Law (Decreto Legislativo No. 708) and its regulations (Decreto Supremo No. 014-92-EM). The Constitution (Art. 66°) guarantees mining rights while requiring environmental protection, implemented through the Environmental Impact Assessment System (Ley No. 27446). Key regulations include the Formalization Law (No. 27651) for artisanal miners, the Mining Royalty Law (No. 29788) establishing 1-12% royalty rates, and the Special Mining Tax Law (No. 29789). The Prior Consultation Law (No. 29785) ensures indigenous community rights under ILO Convention 169, while Supreme Decree No. 028-2018-EM governs social responsibility programs. MINEM (Ministry of Energy and Mines) oversees concession titles valid for 30+ years, with OSINERGMIN regulating technical standards and SUNAT handling tax compliance, including VAT refunds for exporters under Decreto Legislativo No. 1053.

Is infrastructure reliable?

Peru offers well-developed mining infrastructure supported by decades of large-scale operations. The country hosts over 60 major international mining companies including Newmont, Freeport-McMoRan and MMG, with their long-term presence demonstrating operational reliability. Key mineral corridors connect to 15 specialized export ports, primarily Callao and Matarani, which handle 98% of mineral shipments efficiently. The Pan-American Highway system provides critical land access, while dedicated high-voltage transmission lines serve major mining districts. Peru's mining regions benefit from established supply chains with 200+ certified refineries and processing plants, supported by local service providers with decades of industry experience. The government maintains active infrastructure investments through ProInversión, having allocated $8.2 billion to transportation and energy projects since 2020. While remote areas may require temporary logistics solutions, all our permitted projects meet strict accessibility standards verified during the EIA process, with contingency plans for seasonal weather variations. This infrastructure network has successfully supported Peru's position as the world's second-largest copper producer and sixth-largest gold producer, with export volumes growing 22% year-over-year in 2023.
